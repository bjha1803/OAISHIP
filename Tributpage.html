A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

1931-2015

Dr. Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, popularly known as A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, was an eminent Indian scientist and the 11th President of India. He was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India.

Dr. Kalam played a pivotal role in the development of India's space and missile programs. He was a key scientist in the successful Pokhran-II nuclear tests and made significant contributions to India's satellite launch vehicle and ballistic missile technology.

Apart from his scientific achievements, Dr. Kalam was an inspirational figure and a renowned advocate for education, especially among the youth. He strongly believed in the power of knowledge and the potential of young minds to shape a better future.

A.P.J. Abdul Kalam attended the Madras Institute of Technology, where he received a degree in aeronautical engineering in 1960. After graduation he joined the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO)—an Indian military research institute—and later the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO). Kalam’s associations were not limited to research organizations: he was also associated with political groups, such as the National Democratic Alliance (NDA).

Dr. Kalam served as the President of India from 2002 to 2007. During his tenure, he focused on promoting education, information technology, and rural development. He was affectionately referred to as the "People's President" and continued to connect with people from all walks of life even after his presidency.
